OK, so 3rd-party Twitter clients are officially banned, so let’s go over (just a few) of the problems with the official Twitter app:

  1. This blue bubble shows up whenever someone posts a new tweet—which, of course, happens all the time. If you tap it, it jumps all the way to the top of the list (the latest tweets) with no way to go back. This is pointless because on a standard iPhone app you just tap the top of the screen to go to the top. The blue bubble blocks content and there’s no way to get rid of it.

  2. This big plus button is for making a new tweet. It covers your content and it’s HUGE, bigger than the toolbar at the bottom or the top. No way to get rid of it.

  3. All of this junk means there’s only room to see 1 tweet completely. Sheesh.

  4. The “For you/Following” bar is permanently at the top of the screen. Unlike the account/twitter icon bar, it doesn’t slide away when you scroll down and get out of the way. This app sure loves to steal UI space, doesn’t it? This bar should auto-hide somehow at the very least. Better, it shouldn’t be there at all—the “For you” tab is terrible, and isn’t the point of Twitter to see accounts you’re “Following?”

  5. This one is not so bad because it slides out of the way. But the big twitter logo does nothing, and your account avatar opens a secret side menu with a bunch of extra options and views. I’d make the twitter icon on the left and make it open the menu. Your avatar should link to your profile. You could also stick something else up here, like the stupid new tweet button.

  6. What is this icon for? Why is it in one of the most prominent places in the UI?

Lots more going on here, but that’s enough for now!
